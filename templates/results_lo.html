With the amount of free workout videos and virtual 5Ks and home gym products circling social media and advertising right now, it’s okay to feel overwhelmed and a little lost at where to start when it comes to exercising. Maybe you’ve never even thought about exercise before, your job and your social life kept you busy and moving, and you don’t have any idea where to start now that you’re confined to your couch. Maybe you used to hit the gym every day and you’re just not up to that kind of intensity anymore.

Whether it’s as simple as parking far away from the store entrance when you go pick up your groceries or you take a leisurely stroll around your yard or neighborhood or even a local park, walking is a low impact way to get those ol’ bones moving. Just remember to practice safe social distancing habits if you go out in public!

If you’re lucky enough to have a yard, gardening is a great way to enjoy some fresh air and get a light workout without even realizing it. Pulling weeds and potting plants use muscles we don’t typically engage on a normal day. If you’re carrying around full watering cans or squatting down to pick up sticks and other debris, you’ve got yourself almost an entire gym workout!

Yoga can be as low impact and low stress as you want it to be. There are plenty of video guides for all body types, abilities, and skill levels that you can follow along with. Try searching YouTube for some ideas!
